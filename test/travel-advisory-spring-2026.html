Japan — Level 1: Exercise Normal Precautions

Cherry blossom season (late March through mid-April) is peak travel time. No travel restrictions in effect. Expect crowded conditions at popular viewing spots in Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka.

Health: No special vaccinations required. Standard travel insurance recommended.

Currency: JPY trading at 148/USD. Cash still preferred at many traditional establishments.

Southeast Asia — Level 2: Exercise Increased Caution

Monsoon season approaching in Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia (May-October). Flooding risk in low-lying areas. Travel insurance with weather coverage strongly recommended.

Advisory: Avoid coastal areas during storm warnings. Check local weather before island hopping.

Visa: Thailand 60-day visa-free for most nationalities (extended from 30 days in Jan 2026).

Mediterranean — Level 1: Exercise Normal Precautions

Greece, Italy, Spain, and Croatia all operating normally for Spring tourism. EU Digital Travel Certificate no longer required as of January 2026.

Tip: Book accommodations early for Easter week (April 17-21). Prices surge 40-60% in popular destinations.

New: Greece introduced e-visa for non-EU travelers effective February 2026.

East Africa — Level 3: Reconsider Travel (Selected Areas)

Kenya and Tanzania safari season is excellent March-May. However, increased instability near the Kenya-Somalia border. Stick to established tour operators and registered national parks.

Updated: Level raised from 2 to 3 for northern Kenya border region (March 15, 2026).

South America — Level 2: Exercise Increased Caution

Chile and Argentina: normal conditions. Excellent wine regions open for tourism. Patagonia season ending (last month before winter closure).

Colombia and Peru: exercise caution in rural areas. Stick to established tourist routes. Major cities are generally safe.

General Travel Tips — Spring 2026

  • Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage is recommended for all international travel
  • Register with your country's embassy abroad program before departure
  • Keep digital and physical copies of passport, visa, and insurance documents
  • Check visa requirements — several countries updated entry requirements in Q1 2026
  • Download offline maps and translation apps for areas with limited connectivity
